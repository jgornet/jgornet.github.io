How can we better understand the underlying mechanisms in neural systems and neurological diseases?

While the chemical and genetic composition of many neurological phenomena—such as learning and memory—have been largely uncovered, a thorough understanding of the mechanisms in these phenomena has been elusive. This can be partially attributed to the lack of technology necessary to study and understand these aspects. Nevertheless, an understanding of the underlying mechanisms in biological phenomena is becoming increasingly important for understanding the neural system and designing targeted treatments for diseases such as Alzheimer’s, Huntington’s, and ALS.

James Gornet is interested in using his background in biomedical engineering and chemistry to gain a deeper understanding of how different neurological processes occur and how diseases can perturb these processes. More specifically, James Gornet draws from experience in machine learning, computer vision, materials chemistry, and molecular biology to tackle these questions.
